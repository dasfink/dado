# Sauna Construction Sequence

32-step build order for an 8x12 sauna from shed stage. Each step depends on prior steps being complete.

## Foundation and Shell (Steps 1-4)

1. **Site prep** — Grade, 3-5" class 5 gravel, level, layout with string and stakes
2. **Foundation** — Block/pier placement on gravel (preferred over concrete slab)
3. **Floor frame** — 2x6 PT joists 16" OC, rim joists, 2" rigid foam insulation, 3/4" CDX plywood subfloor
4. **Shell** — 2x4 stud walls 16" OC, roof framing, sheathing, roofing (or hire shed company for turnkey shell)

## Interior Rough-In (Steps 5-11)

5. **Seal subfloor** — Kilz/primer paint entire hot room and changing room subfloor
6. **Interior common wall** — Frame at midpoint, 2x4 16" OC, with door rough opening (26" x 77"), leave bottom plate through door opening
7. **Window framing** — Cut openings, frame king/jack/cripple studs, install windows, flash; align candle window and door window heights horizontally
8. **Ceiling framing** — 2x4 or 2x6 rafters at 7' height, 16" OC; end boards at 89", rafters at 66.75"
9. **Chimney rough opening** — 12" x 12" box framed with 2x4s between ceiling rafters; use plumb bob to align with stove position; mark roof with screw
10. **Bench blocking** — Horizontal blocking in wall studs at upper bench cleat height (47.5" down from finished ceiling)
11. **Electrical rough-in** — All boxes, wire runs, speaker wire; extend boxes 3/4" for paneling flush; no outlets in hot room

## Insulation and Vapor Barrier (Steps 12-14)

12. **Insulation** — R-13 batting in all wall cavities, ceiling cavities; cross-layer on top of ceiling rafters; "fluff don't stuff"
13. **Foam seal** — Great Stuff Window & Door (minimal expanding) around all window and door frames; let dry overnight, cut back
14. **Foil vapor barrier** — Complete envelope: first course 48" along floor, second course floor-to-ceiling-to-floor; tape ALL seams with aluminum foil tape

## Stove Area and Floor (Steps 15-21)

15. **Cement board stove surround** — First layer over foil on studs; second layer with 1" copper pipe spacers for air gap; 32" x 32" ceiling piece with chimney hole
16. **Drip edge** — PT 2x6 ripped to ~2"x3", 45-degree chamfer (3/4" x 3/4"), miter corners, secure with adhesive + screws around hot room perimeter
17. **Floor drain** — Cut hole at center of hot room (diagonal corner-to-corner intersection), set 3" x 4" DWV drain with strainer, seal exposed plywood
18. **Patio block stove platform** — 4 x 16" x 16" step stones (32" x 32"), tight against cement board wall, secure perimeter with scrap wood frame
19. **Sleepers** — Tapered PT 2x4 strips (3/4" at drip edge tapering to zero at drain), adhesive + finish nails every 12"; bicycle spoke or concentric square pattern
20. **Cement board floor** — Durock rough side up over sleepers, cut around drain, adhesive on sleepers + 2.5" screws
21. **Skim coat floor** — Vinyl cement repair mixed thin, wet cement board first, pour and trowel; overnight dry minimum

## Paneling and Benches (Steps 22-25)

22. **Paneling** — T&G cedar: back wall first (drip edge to ceiling), then ceiling, then side walls, then front/door wall; tongue-nail at 45 degrees; measure wall in 3 places, subtract 3/16" for expansion
23. **Bench cleats** — Secure through paneling into blocking with screws: back wall cleat (2x4, wall length minus 3"), end cleats (2x4 x 24", doubled to 3" thick)
24. **Build benches** — Picture frame method: 4 long rails + 4 short ends (21") glued and screwed; cross supports ripped to 2" at 1/3 and 2/3 points; 5 deck boards glued and screwed from underneath
25. **Install benches** — Upper on cleats, lower on tracks; triangle corbel supports for spans over 7'

## Finishing (Steps 26-32)

26. **Sauna door** — Build: 1/2" plywood core + 5 vertical 1x6 T&G cedar (inside) + 13 horizontal 1x6 T&G cedar (outside); hang with 2 brass hinges, outswing
27. **Stove installation** — Set on platform with 1/4" shims under feet; install black stove pipe to ceiling support; chimney pipe through roof; flash and seal
28. **Vents** — Adjustable intake (low, near stove) and exhaust (high, opposite corner)
29. **Trim** — Rip T&G tongue off, rip to 2.5" width; door trim with 15-degree angle top piece extending 1/2" past verticals; window trim; cement board transitions
30. **Duck board floor** — Cedar slat pallets (2.5" trim stock on 3/4" x 3/4" rails, 1/2" spacing); two separate pieces for removal/cleaning
31. **Lumbar backrest** — 7 pieces 1x2 cedar on 4 teardrop arch supports from 1x4; mount to wall with 2.5" screws into studs
32. **Finishing touches** — Hooks, changing room shelf (1x4 cedar with L-bracket), door handles, exterior beveled cedar siding, thermometer
