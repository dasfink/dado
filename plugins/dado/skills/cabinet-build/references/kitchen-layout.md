# Kitchen Cabinet Layout Reference

## Work Triangle

The work triangle connects sink, stove, and refrigerator:
- Each leg: 4' to 9'
- Total perimeter: 12' to 26'
- No leg should cross a major traffic path

## Standard Layout Types

### Galley (Two-Wall)
- Two parallel runs, minimum 4' between (42" walkway minimum)
- Sink on one side, stove on the other
- Total run: 8'-12' each wall

### L-Shape
- Two perpendicular walls
- Corner cabinet: 36" x 36" blind corner or lazy susan
- Best for open-plan kitchens

### U-Shape
- Three walls
- Minimum 5' between parallel runs
- Most storage capacity

## Standard Wall Cabinet Widths

Same widths as base cabinets. Heights: 30", 36", or 42" (to ceiling).

## Fillers and Panels

- **Filler strip:** 3" wide solid maple, used between cabinet and wall or between cabinets at corners
- **End panel:** Full 1/4" ply panel to cover exposed carcass side
- **Scribe strip:** 1/4" x 3/4" to cover gap between cabinet and uneven wall

## Countertop Overhang

- **Front:** 1" to 1.5" past face frame
- **Side (exposed):** 1" past end panel
- **Back:** Flush to wall (scribe if needed)
- **Bar/island overhang:** 12"-15" for seating
